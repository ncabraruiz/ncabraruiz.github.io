Nicolás Cabra-Ruiz

I am a pre-doctoral scholar in the Research Program in Development Economics (RPDE) at Princeton University. My interests span political economy, development economics, comparative politics, and peacebuilding studies, where I apply quantitative and qualitative methods to address complex research questions. My research focuses on the intricate dynamics of conflict, migration, electoral behavior and development in Latin America, with a particular emphasis on mass migration, political violence, and governance.

I hold an M.A. in Economics from Universidad de los Andes (Colombia) and a B.A. in Economics from Universidad Nacional de Colombia (UNAL). Additionally, I am completing a second bachelor’s degree in History at UNAL.

Prior to joining Princeton, I worked as a Research Assistant with the World Bank, University of Minnesota, Stanford University, Universidad de los Andes, Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, and the Academia Colombiana de Ciencias Económicas. I also served as a consultant for the Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning Activity (MELA) of USAID in Colombia.

Beyond academia, I enjoy film photography, hiking, theater, and cinema.
